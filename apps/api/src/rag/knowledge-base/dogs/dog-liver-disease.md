## Disease Overview

Liver disease in dogs encompasses a broad spectrum of conditions including acute hepatitis, chronic hepatitis, vacuolar hepatopathy, hepatic lipidosis, cirrhosis, and neoplasia. The liver has significant regenerative capacity, so clinical signs often appear only when substantial hepatic mass is lost. Elevated transaminases (ALT, AST) are the most sensitive early indicators of hepatocellular damage.

## Common Symptoms

- Vomiting, diarrhea
- Anorexia, weight loss
- Polyuria / polydipsia (PU/PD)
- Icterus (yellow mucous membranes, sclera, skin)
- Ascites (advanced disease)
- Neurological signs: hepatic encephalopathy (advanced)
- Coagulopathy: bleeding, petechiae (advanced)

## CBC / Chemistry Patterns

- **Elevated ALT**: Most sensitive and liver-specific marker; indicates hepatocyte injury
- **Elevated AST**: Less specific than ALT (also elevated with muscle damage); parallels ALT in liver disease
- **Elevated ALP**: Cholestasis or steroid-induced hepatopathy; normal ALP elevation expected with glucocorticoid use
- **Elevated GGT**: Complements ALP in biliary or cholestatic disease
- **Elevated TBIL**: Icterus when > 1.5–2.0 mg/dL; reflects cholestasis or severe hepatocellular failure
- **Low ALB**: Hypoalbuminemia indicates chronic or severe disease (liver synthesis failure)
- **Low BUN**: Paradoxically low BUN suggests reduced hepatic urea synthesis — sign of liver failure
- **Low GLU**: Hypoglycemia possible in liver failure, especially in toy breeds

## Interpretation Hints

- ALT elevation alone does not confirm hepatic disease — must correlate with clinical signs and imaging
- ALT:AST ratio helps: if AST elevates disproportionately, consider concurrent muscle disease
- Isolated ALP elevation in a dog on steroids (exogenous or hyperadrenocorticism) may be steroid hepatopathy — not primary liver disease
- Low BUN + low ALB together strongly suggest hepatic synthetic failure
- Cholestasis (high ALP + GGT + TBIL) without high ALT may indicate biliary tree disease rather than hepatocellular damage
- Bile acid stimulation test is valuable when hepatic function is in question but standard enzymes are borderline

## Suggested Next Tests

- **Pre- and post-prandial bile acids** — sensitive test of hepatic function; abnormal before clinical signs
- **Abdominal ultrasound** — evaluates liver size, echogenicity, biliary tree, vascular anatomy (portosystemic shunts)
- **Coagulation profile (PT, aPTT)** — liver produces most coagulation factors; prolonged in severe disease
- **Ammonia level** — elevated in hepatic encephalopathy
- **Urinalysis** — look for bilirubin, ammonium biurate crystals (portosystemic shunts)
- **Liver biopsy** — definitive for type and severity of hepatic disease
- **ACTH stimulation test** — if ALP is markedly elevated and steroid hepatopathy or hyperadrenocorticism suspected
