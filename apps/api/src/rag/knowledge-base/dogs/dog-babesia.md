## Disease Overview

Babesiosis is a tick-borne hemoprotozoan disease caused by _Babesia_ spp. that infect and destroy red blood cells, leading to hemolytic anemia. In Latin America, _B. canis vogeli_ (transmitted by _Rhipicephalus sanguineus_) is the most common form and generally causes milder disease. _B. gibsoni_ can cause more severe, chronic hemolysis.

## Common Symptoms

- Pale or icteric (yellow) mucous membranes
- Weakness, lethargy
- Fever (acute phase)
- Hemoglobinuria (red/brown urine)
- Splenomegaly
- Anorexia
- Vomiting

## CBC / Chemistry Patterns

- **Regenerative hemolytic anemia** (low HCT, HGB, RBC): Hallmark; reticulocytosis expected
- **Thrombocytopenia** (PLT): Concurrent in most cases
- **Hyperbilirubinemia** (elevated TBIL): Due to intravascular/extravascular hemolysis
- **Azotemia** (elevated BUN, CREA): When present, indicates renal involvement — poor prognosis marker
- **Leukocytosis**: Often a stress leukogram
- Mild elevation of **ALT, AST**: Hepatic involvement from hypoxia or hemoglobin toxicity

## Interpretation Hints

- Acute hemolytic anemia + thrombocytopenia + tick exposure = Babesia until proven otherwise
- Icterus in the absence of liver enzyme elevation strongly suggests hemolytic etiology (Babesia)
- Hemoglobinuria (pigmenturia) indicates severe intravascular hemolysis
- Regenerative response (reticulocytes) helps differentiate from aplastic or immune-mediated causes
- B. gibsoni causes chronic, persistent infection; may relapse after treatment

## Suggested Next Tests

- **Blood smear** — piriform (pear-shaped) parasites inside RBCs; sensitivity varies by species
- **PCR for Babesia spp.** — most sensitive and specific; differentiates species
- **Babesia serology (IFA)** — detects antibodies; useful in chronic/subclinical cases
- **Saline agglutination test** — quick in-clinic test for immune-mediated hemolysis (rule out IMH)
- **Urinalysis** — assess for hemoglobinuria, casts (renal involvement)
- **Vector-borne disease panel** — rules out Ehrlichia/Anaplasma co-infection
