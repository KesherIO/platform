## Disease Overview

Canine Parvovirus type 2 (CPV-2) is a highly contagious parvovirus that causes severe hemorrhagic gastroenteritis and bone marrow suppression in dogs. It primarily affects unvaccinated puppies (6 weeks to 6 months) but can infect adults. CPV-2 is environmentally stable and extremely resistant. Mortality without treatment is high. **Severe leukopenia — especially neutropenia — is the hallmark laboratory finding**.

## Common Symptoms

- Severe hemorrhagic vomiting and diarrhea (characteristic foul odor)
- Lethargy, depression
- Anorexia
- Fever (can be hypothermic in shock)
- Rapid dehydration
- Abdominal pain
- Signs of septic shock in severe cases (pale mucous membranes, tachycardia, weak pulse)

## CBC / Chemistry Patterns

- **Severe leukopenia** (WBC): Neutropenia is the hallmark — counts can drop below 1,000/μL; indicates bone marrow suppression
- **Lymphopenia** (LYM): Early and consistent
- **Thrombocytopenia** (PLT): Present in some; DIC (disseminated intravascular coagulation) in the most severe cases
- **Anemia** (HCT, HGB): Progressive with hemorrhagic diarrhea; dehydration may falsely normalize early readings
- **Hypoalbuminemia** (ALB): Protein-losing enteropathy; common and clinically significant
- **Prerenal azotemia** (BUN, CREA): From dehydration; improves with fluid therapy if renal function is intact
- **Hypoglycemia** (GLU): Particularly in young puppies; requires early correction to prevent neurological complications
- **Mild ALT elevation**: Secondary hepatic involvement possible

## Interpretation Hints

- WBC < 2,000/μL in an unvaccinated puppy with hemorrhagic diarrhea = Parvovirus until proven otherwise
- Dehydration can falsely elevate or normalize HCT initially — monitor serially after rehydration
- Hypoglycemia in puppies is an emergency — always check glucose in young dogs with severe GI signs
- Neutropenia predicts risk of secondary bacterial sepsis — indicator for broad-spectrum antibiotic coverage
- Prognosis is better with WBC > 1,000/μL and worsens with persistent neutropenia after 3–5 days of treatment

## Suggested Next Tests

- **Fecal Parvovirus antigen test (ELISA/SNAP)** — highly sensitive and specific; first-line in-clinic test
- **Parvovirus PCR (fecal)** — confirmatory; can detect post-vaccination shedding interference
- **Serial CBC every 24–48 h** — monitor WBC recovery (neutrophil count guide to prognosis)
- **Serial glucose monitoring** — q4–6h in puppies; critical for managing hypoglycemia
- **Serum albumin** — assess degree of protein loss; guides fluid and colloid therapy
- **Coagulation profile (PT, aPTT)** — if DIC suspected (unresponsive hemorrhage, prolonged bleeding)
- **Blood culture** — if persistent fever or deterioration despite treatment (bacterial translocation/sepsis)
