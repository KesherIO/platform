## Disease Overview

Canine Distemper Virus (CDV) is a paramyxovirus causing severe multisystemic disease in dogs, affecting respiratory, gastrointestinal, and nervous systems. It is highly contagious via aerosol droplets. Unvaccinated puppies and young dogs are most at risk. Despite widespread vaccination, CDV remains an important disease in shelter and unvaccinated populations in Latin America. It has a high mortality rate, particularly when neurological signs develop.

## Common Symptoms

**Acute/systemic phase:**

- Fever (biphasic), nasal and ocular discharge (serous → mucopurulent)
- Cough, dyspnea (pneumonia)
- Vomiting, diarrhea
- Anorexia, lethargy

**Later/chronic phase:**

- Hyperkeratosis of footpads and nose ("hard pad disease")
- Enamel hypoplasia (distemper teeth)
- Myoclonus (rhythmic muscle twitching — pathognomonic)
- Seizures, ataxia, paralysis
- Retinal lesions, blindness

## CBC / Chemistry Patterns

- **Lymphopenia** (LYM): Most consistent early finding; CDV is lymphotropic and depletes lymphoid tissue
- **Leukopenia** (WBC): Common in acute phase; indicates lymphoid and bone marrow involvement
- **Thrombocytopenia** (PLT): Less consistent than in tick-borne diseases; possible
- **Neutrophilia** with left shift: Secondary bacterial pneumonia is a common complication
- **Inclusion bodies** (distemper inclusions) may be visible in **RBCs and neutrophils** on blood smear — pathognomonic when present but low sensitivity
- Chemistry panel: generally non-specific; may show hypoalbuminemia or elevated liver enzymes in severe systemic disease

## Interpretation Hints

- Lymphopenia in an unvaccinated young dog with respiratory + GI signs = CDV must be considered
- Inclusion bodies in blood cells on smear are pathognomonic but found in < 30% of cases — negative smear does not rule out CDV
- Myoclonus is considered pathognomonic for CDV when present — a clinical finding, not a lab one
- CDV can cause false-negative Parvovirus tests (lymphopenia masks parvoviral leukopenia profile) — both can co-exist
- Neurological signs without CSF changes do not rule out distemper encephalitis

## Suggested Next Tests

- **CDV antigen rapid test (conjunctival swab or urine)** — quick in-clinic; moderate sensitivity
- **CDV PCR (blood, urine, CSF, conjunctival swab)** — most sensitive; confirms active infection
- **Blood smear** — look for intracytoplasmic inclusion bodies in erythrocytes and leukocytes
- **Chest radiographs** — assess pneumonia severity
- **CSF analysis** — if neurological signs; may show mild pleocytosis
- **Parvovirus antigen test** — rule out co-infection in puppies
