## Disease Overview

Anaplasmosis in dogs is caused by two species: _Anaplasma phagocytophilum_ (infects neutrophils, transmitted by _Ixodes_ ticks) and _Anaplasma platys_ (infects platelets, transmitted by _Rhipicephalus sanguineus_). Both cause thrombocytopenia. _A. phagocytophilum_ also causes significant leukopenia and is clinically more acute.

## Common Symptoms

- Fever, lethargy, anorexia
- Joint pain, stiffness, lameness (_A. phagocytophilum_)
- Hemorrhagic signs: petechiae, ecchymoses (_A. platys_)
- Cyclic thrombocytopenia episodes (_A. platys_)
- Lymphadenopathy
- Vomiting, diarrhea (less common)

## CBC / Chemistry Patterns

- **Thrombocytopenia** (PLT): Present in both species; cyclic pattern in _A. platys_
- **Leukopenia** (WBC): More pronounced with _A. phagocytophilum_; neutropenia + lymphopenia
- **Mild anemia** (low HCT, HGB): Less severe than Ehrlichia or Babesia
- Chemistry panel often within normal limits in uncomplicated cases
- Morulae visible inside **neutrophils** on blood smear (_A. phagocytophilum_) — low but higher sensitivity than Ehrlichia

## Interpretation Hints

- Thrombocytopenia + neutropenia + tick exposure → consider _A. phagocytophilum_
- Cyclic thrombocytopenia in the absence of other signs → consider _A. platys_
- Clinically milder than Ehrlichia in most cases; chronic phase less common
- Co-infection with _Ehrlichia canis_ or Borrelia is possible — consider panel testing
- Morulae in neutrophils (blood smear) are more reliably found early in infection

## Suggested Next Tests

- **Anaplasma serology (IFA or ELISA)** — confirms exposure
- **PCR for Anaplasma spp.** — confirms active infection; differentiates species
- **Blood smear** — look for morulae in neutrophils (_A. phagocytophilum_)
- **Vector-borne disease panel** — rules out Ehrlichia, Babesia, Borrelia co-infections
- **Repeat CBC in 7–14 days** if borderline thrombocytopenia to assess for cyclic pattern (_A. platys_)
