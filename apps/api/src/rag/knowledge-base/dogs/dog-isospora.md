## Disease Overview

Coccidiosis in dogs is caused by _Cystoisospora canis_ and _C. ohioensis_ (previously classified as _Isospora_ spp.). These intracellular protozoa infect the intestinal epithelium. Infection is common in puppies and young dogs, typically via ingestion of oocysts from contaminated environments. Most adult dogs are asymptomatic carriers. Clinical disease is most severe in animals under 6 months of age or those under stress (weaning, transport, crowding).

## Common Symptoms

- Watery to mucoid diarrhea (may contain blood in severe cases)
- Anorexia, lethargy
- Dehydration
- Weight loss and failure to thrive in young puppies
- Generally self-limiting in immunocompetent adult dogs

## CBC / Chemistry Patterns

- **CBC typically within normal limits** in uncomplicated infection
- **Mild leukocytosis** (WBC, NEU): May occur if secondary bacterial enteritis develops
- **Mild anemia** (HCT): Possible secondary to dehydration or prolonged gastrointestinal losses
- **Hypoalbuminemia** (ALB): Only in severe, prolonged infections in young animals

## Interpretation Hints

- Blood work is generally non-diagnostic for Isospora/Cystoisospora — diagnosis is fecal-based
- Diarrhea in puppies from shelters, kennels, or recently weaned litters has high prevalence of coccidiosis
- Stress is a major trigger — immunocompetent adult dogs can be subclinical shedders
- Mixed infections with Giardia, Ancylostoma, or Parvovirus are common in young dogs; evaluate comprehensively
- In immunosuppressed adults, Isospora can cause significant clinical disease

## Suggested Next Tests

- **Fecal flotation (standard)** — detects oocysts; most reliable diagnostic method
- **Fecal direct smear** — useful for rapid examination in clinical setting
- **Fecal PCR** — distinguishes _Cystoisospora_ from _Cryptosporidium_ and other coccidia
- **Full fecal parasite panel** — rules out concurrent Giardia, hookworms, roundworms
- **CBC and serum chemistry** — primarily to assess dehydration severity and guide supportive care
- **Parvovirus antigen test (fecal ELISA)** — rule out concurrent Parvovirus in unvaccinated puppies with severe diarrhea
