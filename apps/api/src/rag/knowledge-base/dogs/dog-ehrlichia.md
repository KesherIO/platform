## Disease Overview

Ehrlichiosis is a tick-borne rickettsial disease caused primarily by _Ehrlichia canis_ in dogs, transmitted by the brown dog tick (_Rhipicephalus sanguineus_). It progresses through three phases: acute, subclinical, and chronic. Thrombocytopenia is the most consistent and early laboratory finding.

## Common Symptoms

- Fever, lethargy, anorexia
- Epistaxis, petechiae, ecchymoses (hemorrhagic signs due to thrombocytopenia)
- Lymphadenopathy, splenomegaly
- Weight loss (chronic phase)
- Ocular discharge, uveitis
- Neurological signs in severe cases

## CBC / Chemistry Patterns

- **Thrombocytopenia** (PLT): Most consistent finding; often severe (< 50,000/μL in acute phase)
- **Anemia** (low HCT, HGB, RBC): Non-regenerative; more pronounced in chronic phase
- **Leukopenia or leukocytosis**: Variable; lymphopenia is common
- **Hyperglobulinemia** (elevated GLOB) with **hypoalbuminemia** (low ALB): Classic chronic pattern
- Mild elevation of **ALT** and **AST** possible
- **Azotemia** (elevated BUN, CREA) if renal involvement present

## Interpretation Hints

- Thrombocytopenia + fever + tick exposure = high suspicion for Ehrlichia
- Chronic phase: hyperglobulinemia combined with hypoalbuminemia is strongly suggestive
- Do not rule out Ehrlichia based on normal WBC — leukogram is variable
- Co-infection with Anaplasma or Babesia is possible and should be considered
- Bone marrow hypoplasia can occur in severe chronic disease

## Suggested Next Tests

- **Ehrlichia serology (IFA)** — reference standard for diagnosis
- **PCR for Ehrlichia canis** — detects active infection, useful in early/acute phase
- **Blood smear** — look for morulae inside monocytes (low sensitivity)
- **Tick panel / vector-borne disease panel** — rules out co-infections (Anaplasma, Babesia, Borrelia)
- **Urinalysis** — assess renal involvement (proteinuria)
- **Bone marrow aspirate** if severe non-regenerative anemia or pancytopenia
