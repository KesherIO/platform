## Disease Overview

Chronic Kidney Disease (CKD) is one of the most common conditions in older dogs. It involves progressive, irreversible loss of functional nephrons. Clinical signs are often absent until > 66–75% of nephron mass is lost. Diagnosis relies on persistent elevation of renal biomarkers (creatinine, BUN) combined with clinical signs and urinalysis. IRIS staging is the standard classification system.

## Common Symptoms

- Polyuria / polydipsia (PU/PD)
- Weight loss, muscle wasting
- Decreased appetite, vomiting
- Lethargy
- Pale mucous membranes (anemia)
- Uremic breath (ammonia odor)
- Oral ulcers (advanced disease)

## CBC / Chemistry Patterns

- **Elevated CREA and BUN** (azotemia): Core diagnostic finding; must be persistent (≥ 2 readings)
- **Elevated PHOS**: Hyperphosphatemia common in IRIS stages 2–4
- **Low ALB**: Hypoalbuminemia in protein-losing nephropathy or advanced disease
- **Non-regenerative anemia** (low HCT, HGB, RBC): Due to reduced erythropoietin production; normocytic, normochromic
- **Elevated SDMA**: May indicate early CKD before creatinine crosses reference range

## IRIS Staging Reference (Creatinine-based)

| Stage | Creatinine (dog) | Notes                              |
| ----- | ---------------- | ---------------------------------- |
| 1     | < 1.4 mg/dL      | Non-azotemic; SDMA may be elevated |
| 2     | 1.4–2.0 mg/dL    | Mild azotemia                      |
| 3     | 2.1–5.0 mg/dL    | Moderate azotemia                  |
| 4     | > 5.0 mg/dL      | Severe azotemia; uremic signs      |

## Interpretation Hints

- A single elevated creatinine does not confirm CKD — dehydration or pre-renal azotemia can elevate it
- Always assess creatinine in context of hydration status and urine specific gravity (USG)
- USG < 1.030 with azotemia suggests primary renal disease (inadequate concentration)
- BUN is less specific than creatinine — high-protein diet, GI bleeding, or catabolism also raise BUN
- Phosphorus elevation is a negative prognostic indicator and should be managed
- Anemia in CKD is typically non-regenerative (no reticulocytosis) — helps differentiate from blood loss or hemolysis

## Suggested Next Tests

- **Urinalysis with USG** — essential; isosthenuria (USG 1.008–1.012) confirms renal insufficiency
- **Urine protein:creatinine ratio (UPC)** — quantifies proteinuria; sub-stages CKD
- **Blood pressure** — hypertension is a common complication and end-organ risk
- **Phosphorus** — if not already included; guides dietary management
- **SDMA** — if not on panel; useful for early detection and staging confirmation
- **Renal ultrasound** — assesses kidney size, architecture, and rules out obstructive causes
- **Urine culture** — rules out concurrent urinary tract infection
