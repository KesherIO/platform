## Pattern Overview

Pale mucous membranes with weakness in a cat is an urgent clinical presentation requiring immediate CBC evaluation. Cats tolerate anemia less visibly than dogs — by the time the owner notices pallor and weakness, anemia is often severe. The three most important causes in cats are _Mycoplasma haemofelis_ (hemotropic mycoplasma), FeLV-associated non-regenerative anemia, and immune-mediated hemolytic anemia (IMHA). Reticulocyte count is essential for distinguishing regenerative from non-regenerative causes.

This document supports AI-assisted triage and test recommendation. It does not constitute a diagnosis.

## Common Symptoms

- Pale to white mucous membranes
- Weakness, collapse, or inability to rise
- Tachycardia, tachypnea (compensatory)
- Lethargy, hiding
- Icterus (yellow tinge) — hemolytic cause
- Splenomegaly on palpation
- Anorexia, weight loss
- History of flea or tick exposure (Mycoplasma haemofelis vector)

## Possible Associated Diseases

| Disease / Pattern                               | Key Differentiating Feature                                                  |
| ----------------------------------------------- | ---------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| _Mycoplasma haemofelis_                         | Regenerative hemolytic anemia; icterus; flea/tick exposure; PCR diagnostic   |
| FeLV-associated non-regenerative anemia         | Non-regenerative; macrocytosis; FeLV antigen positive; pancytopenia possible |
| Immune-mediated hemolytic anemia (IMHA)         | Spherocytosis; positive Coombs; often concurrent with FeLV or other trigger  |
| FIV-associated anemia                           | Chronic non-regenerative; lymphopenia; FIV antibody positive                 |
| Blood loss anemia (trauma, GI ulcer, parasites) | Low TP + ALB; evidence of external or internal bleeding                      |
| Chronic kidney disease with anemia              | Non-regenerative; elevated CREA + BUN; older cat                             |
| Bone marrow suppression (toxic, infiltrative)   | Pancytopenia; non-regenerative; bone marrow aspirate needed                  |

## Recommended Diagnostic Tests

**Immediate:**

- **CBC with reticulocyte count** — severity and regenerative status; most critical first step
- **Blood smear** — look for organisms on RBC surface (_M. haemofelis_); spherocytes; RBC morphology
- **FeLV antigen + FIV antibody test** — mandatory in all anemic cats

**Guided by CBC result:**

- **PCR for _Mycoplasma haemofelis_** — most sensitive method; detects even low parasitemia; recommended over smear alone
- **Direct Coombs test** — if spherocytes or autoagglutination on smear (IMHA)
- **Serum bilirubin (TBIL)** — hemolytic vs. non-hemolytic anemia
- **Serum chemistry + CREA + BUN** — assess concurrent CKD (very common in older cats with anemia)
- **Bone marrow aspirate** — if non-regenerative anemia without FeLV or other clear cause; rules out infiltrative disease

## When to Prioritize Urgent Evaluation

- **HCT < 15%** → blood transfusion consideration — emergency evaluation
- Collapse or inability to maintain sternal recumbency
- Tachycardia at rest or open-mouth breathing from anemia
- White or ghostly pale gums (not just light pink)
- Icterus + pallor together → acute severe hemolysis
- Rapidly developing pallor over hours → active hemolysis or hemorrhage
