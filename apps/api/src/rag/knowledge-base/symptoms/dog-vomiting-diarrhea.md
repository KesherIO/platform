## Pattern Overview

Vomiting and diarrhea in dogs is a very common presentation with a broad differential. The clinical urgency ranges from mild self-limiting dietary indiscretion to life-threatening hemorrhagic gastroenteritis or Parvovirus infection. Patient age, vaccination status, severity of signs, and presence of blood in stool are the most important factors guiding test selection and urgency.

This document supports AI-assisted triage and test recommendation. It does not constitute a diagnosis.

## Common Symptoms

- Vomiting (acute or chronic, with or without food)
- Diarrhea (soft, liquid, mucoid, or hemorrhagic)
- Anorexia, lethargy
- Abdominal pain or guarding
- Dehydration (skin tenting, dry mucous membranes)
- Melena (dark tarry stool) — upper GI bleeding
- Hematochezia (frank red blood in stool) — lower GI or hemorrhagic gastroenteritis
- Weight loss in chronic presentations

## Possible Associated Diseases

| Disease / Pattern                                     | Key Differentiating Feature                                                    |
| ----------------------------------------------------- | ------------------------------------------------------------------------------ |
| Canine Parvovirus (CPV-2)                             | Unvaccinated puppy/young dog; severe leukopenia; bloody diarrhea               |
| Hemorrhagic Gastroenteritis (HGE/AHDS)                | Sudden onset; very high HCT from hemoconcentration; small breed predisposition |
| Dietary indiscretion                                  | Recent dietary change or garbage ingestion; mild-moderate signs                |
| Intestinal parasites (Giardia, Ancylostoma, Isospora) | Young or shelter dogs; pale greasy stools; eosinophilia                        |
| Pancreatitis                                          | Cranial abdominal pain; elevated lipase; history of fatty meal                 |
| Chronic enteropathy / IBD                             | Adult dog; chronic or relapsing; low ALB + TP                                  |
| Foreign body obstruction                              | Persistent vomiting; history of toy/bone ingestion; imaging required           |
| Intussusception                                       | Young dogs; bloody diarrhea + abdominal mass; imaging required                 |
| Parvovirus-like viral gastroenteritis                 | Mild leukopenia; negative Parvo test; supportive care sufficient               |

## Recommended Diagnostic Tests

**First-line:**

- **CBC with differential** — leukopenia = urgent (Parvovirus); HCT guides hydration assessment
- **Fecal parasite exam (flotation + direct smear)** — Giardia, coccidia, hookworms, roundworms
- **Fecal Parvovirus antigen test (SNAP/ELISA)** — mandatory in any unvaccinated or incompletely vaccinated dog with bloody diarrhea or leukopenia

**Second-line (based on first-line results or chronic presentation):**

- **Serum chemistry panel** — BUN, CREA, ALB, TP, ALT, lipase — assess dehydration and pancreatitis
- **Fecal Giardia antigen test** — more sensitive than flotation alone
- **Canine pancreatic lipase (cPLI / Spec cPL)** — most specific test for pancreatitis
- **Abdominal radiographs or ultrasound** — if obstruction, intussusception, or foreign body suspected

**If chronic (> 3–4 weeks):**

- **Serum B12 (cobalamin) and folate** — assess small intestinal function
- **Serum albumin and total protein** — chronic protein-losing enteropathy
- **GI endoscopy + biopsy** — definitive for IBD vs. lymphoma

## When to Prioritize Urgent Evaluation

- Unvaccinated puppy with bloody diarrhea and leukopenia → **Parvovirus — high urgency**
- Severe hematochezia (pure blood in stool) without other explanation
- HCT > 60% with acute onset (HGE/AHDS — rapid fluid therapy needed)
- Blood glucose < 60 mg/dL in a puppy
- Signs of shock: pale gums, tachycardia, weak pulse, hypothermia
- Persistent projectile vomiting without diarrhea → rule out obstruction (imaging urgent)
- Neurological signs alongside GI signs → Parvovirus or other systemic disease
