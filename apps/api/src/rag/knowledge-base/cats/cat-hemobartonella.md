## Disease Overview

_Mycoplasma haemofelis_ (formerly _Haemobartonella felis_) is an obligate intraerythrocytic bacterium that attaches to the surface of feline red blood cells, causing immune-mediated hemolytic anemia. Transmission is via blood-sucking arthropods (fleas, ticks) and possibly through cat fights. It is the most clinically significant hemotropic mycoplasma in cats. Co-infection with FeLV or FIV increases disease severity and is common.

## Common Symptoms

- Pale or icteric mucous membranes
- Lethargy, weakness
- Anorexia, weight loss
- Fever (cyclic, often associated with parasitemia peaks)
- Splenomegaly
- Tachycardia, tachypnea (compensatory, due to anemia)
- In FeLV/FIV co-infected cats: more severe and rapid progression

## CBC / Chemistry Patterns

- **Regenerative hemolytic anemia** (low HCT, HGB, RBC): Most consistent finding; reticulocytosis expected in acute phase
- **Thrombocytopenia** (PLT): Present in some cases
- **Elevated TBIL**: Hemolytic icterus; jaundice visible on mucous membranes and sclera
- **Cyclic anemia**: HCT may fluctuate with parasitemia cycles — repeat CBC may show variation
- Non-regenerative anemia: Possible if concurrent FeLV infection suppresses bone marrow

## Interpretation Hints

- Regenerative anemia + icterus in a cat = high suspicion for M. haemofelis
- Blood smear is positive in acute parasitemia but the organism is not always detectable — PCR is more reliable
- Always test for FeLV and FIV concurrently — these retroviral infections are frequently co-present and worsen prognosis
- Cats can become chronic carriers after treatment and relapse under stress or immunosuppression
- Splenomegaly on palpation or ultrasound supports extravascular hemolysis

## Suggested Next Tests

- **PCR for Mycoplasma haemofelis (blood)** — most sensitive and specific; detects organism even in low parasitemia
- **Blood smear** — look for small, dark organisms on RBC surface; high specificity when positive, low sensitivity
- **FeLV antigen test + FIV antibody test** — essential in all cats with hemolytic anemia
- **Coombs test (direct antiglobulin test)** — rule out primary immune-mediated hemolytic anemia (IMHA)
- **Reticulocyte count** — assess regenerative response
- **Abdominal ultrasound** — evaluate spleen
